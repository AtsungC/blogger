JWST spots biggest water plume yet spewing from a moon of Saturn

The James Webb Space Telescope has only been in orbit for a year, yet it has already played a key role in space exploration. Scientists have discovered possible signs of life on Enceladus, including a plume of icy water spraying from its surface and containing silica, which may originate from the churning of its icy bottom. These findings were spotted by the JWST in just a few minutes. There is much more to expect from the future of the JWST
